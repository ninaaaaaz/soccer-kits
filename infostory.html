A Decade of Shirt Deals

Soccer's top teams have been growing rich on expensive kit and sponsorship agreements.

Average Value of The 20 Most Valuable Teams 2004-2013

August 2002

Manchester United signs 13-year, £303 million Nike kit deal

The Red Devils' 13-year kit deal was still the most lucrative among EPL teams until last summer, and thanks to progressive increases in the annual value it currently pays more than $38 million per year.

July 2003

Juventus gets Nike kit deal for €13 million per season

The 12-year Juve kit deal replaced a three-year contract with Italian sportswear company Lotto.

October 2004

Arsenal picks up 15-year, £100 million Emirates sponsorship deal

Arsenal struck days after rival Chelsea chose not to renew its deal with the Arab airline, locking up a deal that included stadium naming rights and primary shirt sponsorship to begin in the 2006/07 season.

January 2005

Chelsea gets £100 million Adidas kit deal

The West London club paid £24.5 million ($46 million at the time) for an early release from its kit deal with Umbro, which allowed the club to sign with Adidas for £100 million over ten years.

April 2005

Chelsea replaces Emirates sponsorship with one from Samsung

Shortly after adding a rich kit deal with Adidas, Chelsea agreed to a shirt sponsorship deal with South Korean electronics company worth £50 million ($94 million) through five years.

November 2006

Barcelona gets five-year kit deal extension from Nike

Nike and the Catalan club have been partnered since 1998; the five-year extension through 2013 came at a reported price of €150 million ($189 million), or €30 million annually.

2007

Real Madrid nabs bwin shirt sponsorship that pays more than £15 million per year

Europe's top online gambling company became the Spanish club's shirt sponsor in the 2007/08 season after reportedly agreeing to pay upwards of £15 million, or $30 million at the time, annually.

2008

AC Milan and Inter Milan extend kit deals for total €245 million

The two Italian clubs picked up some of soccer's most valuable kit deals within months of each other; Inter's Nike deal pays €14 million/year, while AC Milan's agreement with Adidas is worth €10.5 million/year.

June 2009

Manchester United famously replaces AIG shirt deal with one from Aon

Manchester United flipped one American insurance company for another, picking up a four-year deal with Aon worth £20 million ($32.5 million) per year, a sizable increase from the £14 million AIG was putting up.

September 2009

Liverpool joins £20 million-per-year club with Standard Chartered sponsorship

Liverpool dropped a 17-year Carlsberg sponsorship, then the EPL's longest running shirt deal, in favor of £80 million over four years from Standard Chartered. Carlsberg had been paying just £7.2 million per year.

2010

Barcelona agrees to the team's first ever shirt sponsorship

Barcelona finally accepted its first ever paid shirt sponsorship in 111 years of existence, a €30 million-per-year deal through 2016 with Qatar Sports Investments.

February 2010

AC Milan gets rich shirt sponsorship from Dubai's Emirates Airline

Emirates, one of the world's largest airlines, agreed to a four-year, $82 million deal to replace bwin as AC Milan's shirt sponsor.

October 2010

Chelsea extends Adidas kit deal for more than $30 million annually

The Blues continued their partnership with Adidas on an eight-year extension that could be worth as much as $252 million, or $31.5 million per year.

April 2011

Bayern Munich extends Adidas partnership beyond its 50th year

An eight-year, $289 million extension continued a longstanding and close relationship with Adidas, which also owns a small stake in the team.

July 2011

Manchester City agrees to controversial £400 million sponsorship deal with Etihad

The record 10-year sponsorship deal, which includes stadium naming rights and a shirt sponsorship extension, has drawn ire from those who feel it violates UEFA's financial fair play bylaws.

2012

Real Madrid extends Adidas kit deal; Liverpool gets Warrior

The Spanish side, like Bayern Munich and Chelsea before it, extended its Adidas deal, now worth more than $41 million per year through the 2019/20 season. Liverpool moved on from Adidas, getting a £25 million-per-year kit deal from New Balance’s Warrior brand. The new agreement surpasses Manchester United’s decade-old Nike kit deal as the EPL’s most valuable.

June 2012

Sunderland finds £20 million-per-year support from Tullow Oil-backed non-profit

Non-profit Invest in Africa helped Sunderland become the fourth EPL team to make £20 million per year from a shirt sponsorship, though the charity group is currently reviewing its sponsorship deal.

August 2012

Manchester United agrees to massive new shirt deal with General Motors

A seven-year, $559 million agreement with GM's Chevrolet brand will pay a staggering $70 million in the 2014/15 season alone. Annual payments rise by more than 2% annually through 2020/21.

March 2013

Barcelona's Qatar sponsorship replaces charity logo with Qatar Airways

Qatar Sports Investments exercised an option in the company's shirt deal to replace Qatar Foundation with Qatar Airways, the first non-charity sponsor in Barcelona's history, at a reported cost of $45 million per year.

April 2013

Manchester United currently renegotiating its Nike kit deal

A six-month negotiating period began in February, and the new contract is widely expected to be the richest kit deal in the history of the sport.
